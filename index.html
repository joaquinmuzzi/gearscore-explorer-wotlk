How is GearScore calculated?

A GearScore table assigns a score to an item based on its item level and equipment slot.

For each slot type, GearScore can be approximated using a linear model:

GS(ilvl, slot) ≈ a_slot · ilvl + b_slot

Where GS is the estimated GearScore contribution of the item, ilvl is the item level, and a_slot and b_slot are coefficients that depend on the equipment slot (High-slot, Mid-slot, Low-slot, Ranged, Two-Hand).

The coefficient a_slot represents how much GearScore increases per item level for that slot, while b_slot is a constant offset used to fit the approximation to the real data.

Example (High-slot): using the points (ilvl = 200, GS = 310) and (ilvl = 284, GS = 551), a linear fit gives a ≈ 2.87 and b ≈ −264.

In practice, many GearScore implementations also apply additional scaling based on item quality and predefined slot weights. The values shown here are approximations; the exact GearScore values are taken from the internal ILVL_GS table.

Approximate coefficients by slot type:

  • High-slot: a ≈ 2.87, b ≈ −264
  • Mid-slot: a ≈ 2.14, b ≈ −196
  • Low-slot: a ≈ 1.62, b ≈ −150
  • Ranged: a ≈ 0.90, b ≈ −83
  • Two-Hand: a ≈ 5.74, b ≈ −527
